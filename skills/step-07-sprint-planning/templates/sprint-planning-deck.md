# Sprint Planning Facilitation Slide Deck

This presentation guide helps the Scrum Master facilitate the Sprint Planning ceremony, ensuring the team aligns on the Sprint Goal, reviews capacity, and commits to the Sprint Backlog.

---

## 📽️ SLIDE 1: Welcome & Sprint Overview
*   **Sprint Name/Number:** Sprint [N]
*   **Sprint Dates:** [Start Date] to [End Date] (Duration: [X] Weeks)
*   **Facilitator:** [Scrum Master Name]
*   **Ceremony Timebox:** [e.g., 2 hours for a 2-week Sprint]
*   **Agenda:**
    1.  Why is this Sprint valuable? (Draft Sprint Goal)
    2.  What can be Done in this Sprint? (Forecast PBIs)
    3.  How will the chosen work get done? (Decompose Tasks)

---

## 📽️ SLIDE 2: Team Capacity & Velocity Baseline
*   **Historical Velocity:**
    *   *Last 3 Sprints:* [Sprint N-3: X pts] | [Sprint N-2: Y pts] | [Sprint N-1: Z pts]
    *   *Average / Trend:* [Average Velocity] points
*   **Sprint [N] Team Capacity:**
    *   *Available Developers/QAs:* [Number of members]
    *   *Planned Time Off / Holidays:* [Days off of team members]
    *   *Focus Factor / Calculated Capacity:* [Calculated capacity in points or hours]

---

## 📽️ SLIDE 3: Why is this Sprint Valuable? (Sprint Goal)
*   **Proposed Product Goal Context:** [Reference Current Product Goal]
*   **Draft Sprint Goal:**
    > *"Focus on [specific customer value or outcome] to enable [impact or benefit]."*
*   **Key Question for Developers:** *"Is this Sprint Goal clear, and does it leave room for flexibility in implementation?"*

---

## 📽️ SLIDE 4: What can be Done? (Sprint Backlog Selection)
*   **Forecasted Product Backlog Items (PBIs):**
    
    | Order | PBI Title | Story Points | Priority / Rationale |
    |---|---|---|---|
    | 1 | [PBI-101: e.g., Integrate payment checkout] | [5 pts] | Core Goal |
    | 2 | [PBI-102: e.g., Add security limits check] | [3 pts] | Compliance / Core Goal |
    | 3 | [PBI-103: e.g., Fix wallet database lock issue] | [3 pts] | Tech Debt / Priority |
    | **Total** | | **[11 pts]** | |

*   **Confidence Poll:** *"Scale of 1–5, how confident are we that we can deliver this forecast?"*

---

## 📽️ SLIDE 5: How will it get Done? (Task Decomposition)
*   *Developers decompose the selected PBIs into daily tasks (ideally ≤ 1 day of work).*
*   **PBI 101 Tasks:**
    *   [ ] Task 1.1: Design checkout UI layout (Owner: [Name], Est: [X]h)
    *   [ ] Task 1.2: Implement transaction API integration (Owner: [Name], Est: [Y]h)
    *   [ ] Task 1.3: Run manual integration test in sandbox (Owner: [Name], Est: [Z]h)
*   **PBI 102 Tasks:**
    *   [ ] Task 2.1: ...

---

## 📽️ SLIDE 6: Alignment & Formal Commitment
*   **Definition of Ready (DoR) Check:** Do all selected PBIs meet our DoR?
*   **Definition of Done (DoD) Check:** Are we clear on the quality gates (Code review, QA testing, Compliance checks)?
*   **Final Alignment Statement:**
    > *"We, the Scrum Team, commit to executing the Sprint Backlog and making our best effort to achieve the Sprint Goal of Sprint [N]."*
*   **Socratic Closure:** *"Does anyone see any major impediment that might block us from achieving this Sprint Goal in the first 3 days?"*
